Born in Seoul, Korea, I immigrated to the States at a young age where I moved from city to city in California before finally settling down in Los Angeles. While deeply attached to the cultural roots of my native country, growing up in California allowed me to experience changes in my perspectives toward the social and cultural values shared within the world, and also to form a perception of my own identity. I hold on to my childhood memories as a way to define my root and identity. These cherished moments, places, and things that stay vividly with me throughout the years of changes often inspire my work. Using a combination of traditional and modern techniques and styles, I enjoy making figurative illustrations and incorporating metaphoric imagery to convey the messages. I regularly find myself attracted to feelings of nostalgia, and they often appear in my work through the colors and figures.
